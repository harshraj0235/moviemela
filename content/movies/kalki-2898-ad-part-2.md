## Story

The story of *Kalki 2898 AD Part 2* will pick up exactly where the first film left off. The climax of Part 1 was a cinematic spectacle that left audiences breathless. Bhairava (Prabhas) was revealed to be the reincarnation of Karna, wielding the Vijaya Dhanush in a brief moment of awakened memory, fighting alongside Ashwatthama. 

In Part 2, the narrative will deeply explore the complex dynamic between Bhairava and Ashwatthama. Will Bhairava fully realize his past life as Karna? Will he join forces with Ashwatthama to protect the child, or will his selfish bounty-hunter nature pull him back to the Complex?

Furthermore, Supreme Yaskin is now a much deadlier threat. Having consumed the extracted serum, he is no longer a fragile old man suspended in the air. He is a formidable warrior with god-like strength. The sequel will focus on the ultimate clash between the forces of Shambhala and the Complex, culminating in the birth and rise of the Kalki avatar to end the Kali Yuga.

## Director

**Nag Ashwin**, the visionary director behind *Mahanati*, has cemented his place as one of India's most ambitious filmmakers. His ability to seamlessly blend ancient Hindu mythology with futuristic dystopian sci-fi is unparalleled in Indian cinema. For Part 2, Ashwin has promised an even darker, more action-packed narrative that expands the universe far beyond the city of Kasi and the Complex.

## Producer

The film is produced by **C. Aswani Dutt** under the banner of **Vyjayanthi Movies**, one of the most prestigious and historic production houses in Tollywood. Celebrating their 50th anniversary with the *Kalki* franchise, the producers have spared no expense in ensuring the film matches Hollywood standards in terms of visual effects and production design.

## Budget

The first part of *Kalki 2898 AD* was made on a staggering budget of approximately ₹600 Crores, making it one of the most expensive Indian films ever produced. For the sequel, the stakes are even higher. 

Industry analysts predict the budget for *Kalki 2898 AD Part 2* will comfortably exceed **₹750 to ₹800 Crores**. A massive portion of this budget will be allocated to international VFX studios to render the colossal final battle sequences and the expanded world of Shambhala. Additionally, the cast remunerations and extensive marketing campaigns across multiple languages will contribute heavily to the cost.

## Filming Location

While the first film was heavily shot on meticulously crafted sets in Ramoji Film City, Hyderabad, the sequel is expected to expand its horizons. Reports suggest that the team will shoot in real locations featuring harsh, barren landscapes to represent the wasteland outside Kasi, potentially filming in the deserts of the Middle East or North Africa. The highly guarded interior sets of the Complex and Shambhala will continue to be filmed in Hyderabad.

## Music

**Santhosh Narayanan**, who provided the thunderous and epic background score for the first film, is confirmed to return. His unique blend of futuristic electronic synths with traditional Indian classical instruments created a distinct sonic identity for the *Kalki* universe. Fans can expect an even more bombastic score, especially for the high-octane sequences involving Supreme Yaskin and the Karna flashbacks.
